## SoilProve Overview

Source: 3\. SoilProve \- Precision Nutrient Mgt & Fertilizer Optimization/00\_SoilProve Overview.pdf

SoilProve Field-specific nitrogen recommendations, proven by your neighbors.

The Problem Nitrogen fertilizer is one of the largest input costs for corn farmers — and one of the least precisely managed. Farmers in Iowa, Illinois, and Indiana are routinely applying nitrogen at rates that are either too high (wasting money, contributing to runoff, and degrading soil health) or too low (suppressing yield and revenue). Generic county-level or statewide recommendations don't account for field-specific soil variability, and the agronomists and co-ops offering nitrogen guidance often have financial ties to the fertilizer products they recommend, creating a trust deficit that makes farmers skeptical of the advice they receive.

The Solution SoilProve is a B2B SaaS platform that generates field-specific nitrogen fertilizer prescriptions using the Midwest Nitrogen Rate Trial (MRTN) methodology — the most scientifically credible framework available for corn nitrogen optimization. What makes SoilProve distinctive is a peer-validation layer: recommendations are cross-referenced against data from neighboring farms in the same county and soil type, so farmers can see that their prescription reflects what's actually working nearby, not just a model output. After each season, the platform tracks measurable savings per acre against the farmer's prior practices, generating a verified ROI figure that compounds trust and retention over time.

Target Market & Go-to-Market SoilProve's early beta targets 20–30 corn farmers across Iowa, Illinois, and Indiana, with a focus on operations large enough that a $15–25 per-acre savings represents meaningful annual value. Rather than relying on paid acquisition, SoilProve's go-to-market strategy centers on agronomist partnerships — recruiting independent agronomists as channel partners who can introduce the platform to their existing farmer relationships — and peer referrals that leverage the network effects of the peer-validation data model. This approach keeps customer acquisition costs low while building the data density that makes the platform more accurate over time.

Why This Wins The intersection of scientific credibility (MRTN methodology), social proof (peer-validated recommendations from neighboring farms), and measurable outcomes (tracked savings per acre) creates a trust stack that generic fertilizer recommendations and vendor-sold tools cannot replicate. Every season of data makes SoilProve's prescriptions more precise and its peer network more valuable, building a defensible data moat that strengthens with scale.
